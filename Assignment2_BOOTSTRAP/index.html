Spiritual but not religious

(SBNR) is a popular phrase and initialism used to self-identify a life stance of spirituality that takes issue with organized religion as the sole or most valuable means of furthering spiritual growth. Spirituality places an emphasis upon the wellbeing of the "mind-body-spirit,"[95] so "holistic" activities such as tai chi, reiki, and yoga are common within the SBNR movement.
